Historical Torino Players

Grande Torino: The Grande Torino, played in the 40's, dominating the Italian league, winning 5 championships in a row, from 1943 to 1949. The eleven players who dominated the league were: Bagicalupo, Ballarin, Maroso, Grezar, Rigamonti, Castigliano, Menti, Loik, Gabetto, Mazzola, Ossola. The Italian National team was composed by ten Grande Torino players of the eleven National team players, something done by no other team in the world. Torino's captain Valentino Mazzola, was invited to play a game against his good friend's team Benfica. After this game In Portugal, they were on their way back to Turin. That day was filled with darkness, rain, and fog, which heavily impacted the pilots. In a horrible and tragic moment, all players of the Grande Torino crashed and died. May 4th, 1949, will be remebered as one of Torino's darkest days, but also creates a legend for Torino history. Gigi Meroni: Gigi Meroni, or in italian the "Farfalla Granata" which translates to the maroon butterfly, was a Torino icon. He had unbelievable individual skills and qualaties. He was so good with the ball, he was an artist on the field, he could do things no one else could. Unfortunatly, at age 24 his unbelievable talent was ended on October 15th, 1967, where he was killed while crossing the street in Turin. Strangley the driver of the car later became a future Torino owner. He is truly, a Torino legend for ever for what he did on the field. Paolo Pulici: Paolo Pulici is another amazing Torino star. Pulici, was aquired by Torino in 1967, and grew in Torino's youth teams. Pulici was a true goal scoring machine, he earned the name "Puliciclone" or the cyclone, for his immense power and strength.
